Contrast

It is essential to establish contrast between distinct elements on a webpage, whether it is colour, size, or form, so that each element can uniquely stand out from the rest. Contrast not only helps the user distinguish between elements, but also improves page readability and navigation.

Repetition

Well used repetition can provide a webpage with consistency and flow. Repeated use of elements with a common property creates a satisfying visual pattern and a sense of comfort. Although, it is important to provide variation with little adjustments, in order to pique the user’s inquisitiveness and avoid a webpage becoming monotone.

Alignment

Proper alignment helps to provide structure, and can be the difference between a professional, or an amateur hour webpage. A user may not always notice alignment is present in good web design, but they will certainly notice when it is not. Used effectively, alignment can create a compelling blueprint for the user’s eye to travel, and significantly impact the overall aesthetic of the design.

Proximity

At face value, effective use of proximity is required to reduce the look of clutter, and through the strategic use of white space to produce a comprehensive and professional webpage. Equally as important, proximity is used to establish harmonious relationships between distinct elements, or to isolate them, by creating meaningful groupings that convey significance to the user.
